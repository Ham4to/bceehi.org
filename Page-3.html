A BEAM OF HOPE PROJECT 

A BEAM OF HOPE PROJECT 

is an initiative of BCEEHI Africa designed to tackle the pressing challenges that prevent children from deprived backgrounds in Ghana from accessing quality education. The project focuses on Answaru-Deen Islamic Basic School in New Fadama, Accra, Ghana, as its starting point, with plans to expand to other deprived communities in the country. Despite government interventions like the Free Compulsory Universal Basic Education (FCUBE) policy, poverty continues to be a major barrier to education for many Ghanaian children. This project will provide backpacks, school supplies, hygiene products, computers/laptops/tablets and scholarships to help students remain in school and focus on their education. Numerous children in Ghana face significant challenges, such as the lack of basic school supplies and hygiene products, which directly affect their ability to attend school regularly. Research shows that students who feel equipped and confident are more likely to engage in their education and perform better academically (Ref: UNICEF, 2021). Additionally, the burden of poverty forces some children into child labor, leaving them unable to fully concentrate on their education. A BEAM OF HOPE PROJECT aims to address these barriers by ensuring that students have access to the necessary resources to stay in school. By alleviating these challenges, the project seeks to break the cycle of poverty and open doors to a brighter future for these children.

Project Objectives:  
  • Provide essential school supplies (bags, notebooks, stationery) to 480 students biannually.  
  •  Supply feminine hygiene products to 257 girls twice a year to ensure consistent school attendance. 
  • Offer scholarships and financial aid to at least 50 students from deprived backgrounds, enabling them to stay in school and focus on their studies.  
  • Engage the local community, including parents and leaders, to raise awareness about the importance of education and build local support for sustained impact.

4. Scalability and Replication: The challenges faced by Answaru-Deen Islamic Basic School are representative of many other deprived communities across Ghana.
